IPHAS Data Access

IPHAS provides imaging data and an associated source catalogue, along with derived data products such as a 3D extinction map. The most recent Data Release 2 (DR2) is described in Barentsen et al (2014). The primary data products are:

ProductLinkDescription
Imaging iphas.org/images Pipeline-processed CCD images in the r, i, and Hα-bands, covering the entire northern Milky Way.
Catalogue iphas.org/dr2 Photometry and astrometry for 219 million objects detected in the imaging.
Extinction map iphas.org/extinction A 3D map of interstellar dust extinction derived from the catalogue.
Symbiotic stars iphas.org/symbiotics Positions and spectra of symbiotic stars discovered in IPHAS data.

Acknowledging IPHAS data

Publications arising from IPHAS data should cite and acknowledge the survey. The primary reference for defining the survey is Drew et al 2005 [bibtex]. Where the data have come from the catalogues or images released as part of DR2, Barentsen et al 2014 [bibtex] must be cited as well.

Here is text to be used in acknowledgment sections of papers:
